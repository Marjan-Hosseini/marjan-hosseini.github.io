About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Connecticut, working at the intersection of computational biology, Bayesian machine learning, and large-scale data analysis. My work focuses on modeling high-throughput sequencing data (e.g., nanopore), building end-to-end ML pipelines, and designing probabilistic models that quantify uncertainty in noisy biological signals.

I have over a decade of experience in research and engineering roles spanning academia and industry, including work on feature selection for high-dimensional genomics, GPU performance modeling, cloud optimization, and signal/image processing for smart devices.
